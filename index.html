Impact of Post-War Immigration on Australia

Exploring the legacy and changes in immigration policies

Post-war immigration significantly reshaped Australia's population and cultural landscape, as the country welcomed large numbers of migrants to boost its workforce and economy. This period eventually led to the dismantling of the White Australia Policy, marking a shift toward a more multicultural society.

Post-war immigration was a major part of what influenced the change of the immigration laws, and subsequently, “White Australia”. One of the related laws was the Immigration Act, it was a law in Australia, although spread across different countries that regulated and tightened the restrictions of who was allowed in. Based on nationality, skills or family connections. The immigration act was introduced in December of 1901, it was actually one of the first laws passed the new federal parliament after Australia's federation. One of the ways they got people to vote was through propaganda and speechs from members of the parliament. An example would be John "The Big Fella" Thomas Lang, a former member of parliament (from 1925 -> 1927 & 1930 -> 1932)

John "The Big Fella" Thomas Lang

Bad immigration laws targeted non-European immigrants, especially Asian and Pacific Islander immigrants by putting harsh restrictions on their entry. An example would be the dictation test, which was introduced in a policy similar to White Australia which required potential immigrants to write a piece of writing using a European language. It was mainly used to purposely exclude people even if they met all the other requirements and limited non-European immigration. It impatced the migrants negatively due to the fact that it meant they weren't able to flee their countries in case of conflict, political overrule and other forms of bad things in their home country. However one alleged upside of the White Australia policy is that higher wagers were in place due to less workers, because of a lower population meaning people got paid more. Which is statistically better for the economy and housing prices.

The White Australia policy was ended due to people's views on race changing. It was influenced by some other countries and the need for a better workforce. It led to more inclusive immigration that let non-European immigrants settle into Australia. It also allowed immigrants to have new chances for work, schooling although racism still existed. Some citizens didn't like the new changes and some feared change in culture and harder times getting work. But others thought it was good. One of the key causes of the change was also through the Whitlam Government. In 1973 the Whitlam Labor Government who definetively renounced the White Australia policy and put a policy of multiculturalism in its place to help popularise multiculturalism. Some people didn't like it due to the fact that one of the reasons they opened the borders being for cheap labor. More immigrants = higher population = more workers = lower wages = cheap labor

Today, Australia is one of the most multicultural countries in the world, with a mix of languages, traditions, and communities that shape its identity, economy, and values.
